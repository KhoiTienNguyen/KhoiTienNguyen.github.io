What this trip offers

One country.
Three different rhythms.

Every route includes Skye, Glencoe and major Highland scenery. Route 1 keeps going north for the biggest mountain-and-coast journey. Route 2 exchanges northern mileage for Mull, Iona and ferries. Route 3 protects more time for forest walks, cycling and Glen Affric.

The maps show the shape; the galleries show what you would actually see.

At a glance

Shared roads, shown side by side.

Read the overview like a subway map. Parallel colors mean the routes use the same corridor. Arrows show travel direction, and the route names sit directly on the map so the legend is never doing all the work.

Orientation: Edinburgh is just north-east of Glasgow; Inverness and the Cairngorms are farther north. The route lines follow that geography.

The choice

Three ways north.

Choose by the kind of days you want, not by the longest attraction list. Each route below has a directional map, a day-by-day visual gallery, a clear first cut, and its own outdoor add-on.

Route 01 · 11-13 days

Classic Highlands + Skye + the far north-west

The biggest landscape route: Glencoe, Skye, Applecross, Torridon and Assynt. Choose it when the goal is the most dramatic roads and mountains, and you are happy with longer driving days.

Route 02 · 10-12 days

West coast + Mull + Skye

The most ocean-focused route. It trades some remote north-west mileage for Mull, Iona, ferry crossings, harbour towns and a softer pace around Skye.

Extra days

Use an extra Mull day for Iona or Staffa conditions, or add a third Skye night.

Three nights is the useful minimum. Split the island into distinct areas and let the forecast decide their order.

A simple three-day structure

One island.
Three different landscapes.

  1. Trotternish: Storr, Quiraing, waterfalls and Staffin.
  2. Cuillin and south: Fairy Pools, Sligachan, Elgol and Loch Coruisk.
  3. North-west coast: Dunvegan, Coral Beach, Waternish and Neist Point.
Day one · Trotternish

Start with the two defining walks.

Do Storr or the Quiraing early, not both as rushed photo stops. Storr is shorter and steeper; the full Quiraing loop takes longer and feels more varied. Use the roadside waterfalls and Staffin as flexible stops between them.
