The editorial call

Choose a shape, not a checklist.

For a 9–13 day trip, Scotland deserves to be the whole story. West England is a beautiful future chapter, but adding it here would trade away precisely what you came for: unhurried Skye days, road-weather improvisation and the quiet after the big views.

Early September is a strong window: long enough days for real scenic driving, the first hint of autumn colour, and a livelier, softer road trip than a pure mileage challenge.

How to use this magazine

Each numbered route is a day-by-day menu. Plan for two anchor experiences plus a few small stops—not every pin. Solid pills are the rhythm; dashed pills are where you stretch, linger, or cut.

02 / The common centrepiece

Give Skye the
weather it deserves.

Every route converges here. The most useful decision is not “what can we see?” but “which side of the island gets the best weather window?” Aim for two full days at minimum; three nights gives you freedom.

North / Trotternish

Rock theatre

Old Man of Storr, Lealt, Kilt Rock, Quiraing, Staffin and Fairy Glen. Start early; let one headline walk be enough.

West / Cuillin

Water & mountain

Fairy Pools, Sligachan, Glenbrittle, Talisker and Elgol. Keep the long outdoor day for the clearest forecast.

North-west / coast

Slow sunset

Dunvegan, Coral Beach, Waternish and Neist Point. This is the best lane for a long lunch and late light.

A route can change without falling apart. These are the cleanest edits—ones that preserve the character rather than simply deleting days.

04 / Pocket expeditions

When the road needs a pause.

Walk

Glencoe: choose Hidden Valley for a compact, high-drama mountain-path outing. Save more serious terrain for a calm forecast and the right gear.

Ride

Cairngorms: make Loch an Eilein and the Old Logging Way a deliberate easy day. It breaks the driving pattern beautifully.

Sleep out

Affric / Kintail: a section of the Affric Kintail Way is the most natural overnight flavour. Lightweight camping only, with conditions and local advice checked close to departure.
